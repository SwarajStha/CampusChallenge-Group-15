Use these rules for the classification:

Your sentiment score should be a single number between -1 and 1 (inclusive) with up to two decimal places, representing your confidence in the price movement direction. Use this scale:
  • 0.70 to 1.00 = Strong buy signal (very confident the stock will rise significantly). Major Positive: Significant earnings beats, major contracts, strategic M&A, positive regulatory wins, or strong revenue growth.
  • 0.30 to 0.69 = Moderate buy signal (somewhat confident the stock will rise). General Positive: Good news with slight uncertainty, modest earnings beats, solid guidance, or product launches with good initial reception.
  • 0.01 to 0.29 = Weak buy signal (slight positive tilt). Minor Positive: Small positive developments, mixed news with a positive bias, or general sector optimism affecting the stock.
  • 0.0 = Neutral/hold (no expected movement). Ambiguous/Flat: Routine updates, mixed news with no clear direction, noise not directly related to stock performance, or priced-in information.
  • -0.01 to -0.29 = Weak sell signal (slight negative tilt). Minor Negative: Small setbacks, delays, mixed news with a negative bias, or slight underperformance against minor metrics.
  • -0.30 to -0.69 = Moderate sell signal (somewhat confident the stock will fall). General Negative: Missed earnings, negative guidance, minor recalls, executive departures, or increased competitor pressure.
  • -0.70 to -1.0 = Strong sell signal (very confident the stock will fall significantly). Major Negative: Severe distress, major losses, large-scale recalls, SEC investigations, fraud allegations, or bankruptcy risks.

Additional classification aid:
Risk sensitivity: High uncertainty should reduce confidence (move closer to 0).
News type hints: Earnings surprises, guidance changes, downgrades, upgrades, regulatory actions deserve stronger signals; marketing campaigns, minor updates deserve weaker signals.
Calibrated ambiguity handling: Questions in headlines ("Is X sustainable?") should be scored near 0 unless clear consensus is implied.
Tag interpretation: Use the tags to understand the article's focus. Tags like 'earnings', 'bankruptcy', 'FDA', 'acquisition' suggest high-impact news. Tags like 'marketing', 'opinion', 'analyst-commentary' suggest lower impact.
